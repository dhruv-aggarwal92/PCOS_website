Understanding PCOS

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common hormonal disorder that affects women of reproductive age. It can cause a range of symptoms such as irregular periods, excessive androgen levels (male hormones), and polycystic ovaries. Understanding the condition and taking proactive steps can help in managing its symptoms and long-term effects.

Symptoms of PCOS

  • Irregular menstrual cycles
  • Excessive hair growth (hirsutism)
  • Weight gain or difficulty losing weight
  • Acne and oily skin
  • Thinning hair or hair loss
  • Infertility or difficulty conceiving

Long-term Health Risks

  • Type 2 diabetes Caused by insulin resistance associated with PCOS.
  • High blood pressure Linked to obesity and hormonal imbalance.
  • Heart disease Higher risk due to metabolic issues.
  • Endometrial cancer Resulting from irregular periods and hormonal imbalance.
